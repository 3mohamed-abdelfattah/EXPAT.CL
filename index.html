Still Have Questions?

Find the answers here about your residency in Chile.

What type of visa do I need to relocate to Chile? +
The type of visa you need depends on your specific situation. Common options include work visas, retirement visas, and investor visas. Our visa quiz is designed to quickly help you identify which visa best fits your personal circumstances.
How long does the visa application process typically take? +
The process duration depends on the visa type and documentation provided. Typically, it takes a few weeks to a few months.
Do I need to have a job offer before applying for residency? +
While some visas require a job offer, others, like investor or retirement visas, do not.
Can my family members apply with me? +
Yes, most visa types allow family members to apply as dependents.
Can I apply for my visa while in Chile as a tourist? +
In many cases, you can apply for a visa while in Chile as a tourist, but it's best to verify specific requirements.
What happens if my visa application is denied? +
If your application is denied, you may appeal or reapply with the required changes.
Can I work in Chile while waiting for my visa? +
It depends on the visa type. Some visas allow provisional work permits while waiting for approval.
How much does the visa process cost? +
Costs vary depending on the visa type and processing fees. It's best to check with the official immigration office for updated fees.
